GENESIS

A band defined as much by who left as by who stayed — five became three across a single restless decade.

15 studio albums 11 members across the years 2 constant from first to last: Banks & Rutherford
01

The members

Eleven musicians passed through Genesis, but only keyboardist Tony Banks and bassist/guitarist Mike Rutherford were present from the school-band beginnings to the final record. The two singers who fronted the band — Peter Gabriel, then Phil Collins — bracket its two great eras.

Chris Stewart · John Silver · John Mayhew
Pre-Collins, 1967–1970

On the early drum stool: Chris Stewart played on the band's 1968 debut single, John Silver drummed on the first album, and John Mayhew on the second — each leaving before the next. Guitarist Mick Barnard also held the chair briefly in 1970 between Phillips and Hackett. Touring members who shaped the live sound but never appeared on a studio album include drummer Chester Thompson, guitarist/bassist Daryl Stuermer, and — on the 2021–22 farewell — Phil's son Nic Collins on drums.

Life beyond the band

Gabriel became an art-rock auteur, Collins one of the biggest pop stars of the 80s, Rutherford built a hit machine in Mike + The Mechanics, Banks turned to orchestral composition, and Hackett never stopped — clocking up around thirty solo records. YES

Seventeen players, twenty-four albums, a sound forever chasing the edge — and one bassist who held it all together until the end.

24 studio albums 17 players across them 1 near-constant: Chris Squire, on every album until 2015
01

The members

Yes turned the revolving-door line-up into an art form. Bassist Chris Squire was the one fixed point, playing on every studio album from 1969 until his death in 2015; everyone else — three lead singers, six keyboardists, a procession of guitarists and drummers — came, went, and often came back.
